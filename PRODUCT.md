# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

A single user (the owner) managing their own personal finances, calendar/study schedule, and a study-assistant chat, all in one private workspace. No other accounts, no multi-tenant use, no login screen — this is a personal tool, used by one person, on their own devices.

## Product Purpose

A personal dashboard consolidating three areas of the user's life: budgeting/expense tracking (transactions, savings goal, subscriptions), a calendar with recurring class schedules and study/exam/deadline events, and a study module. Success looks like the user opening it daily and trusting the numbers and schedule at a glance, without it feeling like generic dashboard software.

## Brand Personality

Confident & structured. Clear hierarchy and data-forward presentation, with enough visual personality that it reads as a deliberately crafted personal tool rather than neutral utility software.

## Anti-references

No generic SaaS look: avoid the templated dashboard-generator aesthetic (gradient text, hero-metric cards, uniform icon+heading+text card grids, tiny uppercase eyebrows on every section). Existing structural elements (sidebar nav, topbar, forms, tables) are the right shape for the workflow — the anti-reference is about visual treatment (color, type, motion, card overuse), not about ripping out that structure.

## Design Principles

- Confidence over neutrality — commit to a defined color and type identity instead of defaulting to safe gray SaaS neutrals.
- Structure carries the personality — hierarchy, spacing, and type do the identity work before decoration does.
- Clarity is non-negotiable — this manages real money and a real schedule; brand expression never compromises legibility or data accuracy.
- No borrowed templates — every screen should look considered for this specific dashboard, not assembled from generic SaaS/dashboard-generator patterns.
- One workspace, one voice — finance, calendar, and study should feel like facets of the same crafted tool, not three bolted-together modules.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: ≥4.5:1 body text contrast, ≥3:1 for large text, keyboard-navigable interactive elements, and `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all animation.
